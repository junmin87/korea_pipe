# Korean Question Words: Who, What, When, Where, Why, How

To ask questions in Korean, you need the **interrogatives** — the words for who, what, when, where, why, and how. The good news: Korean keeps the same word order as a statement, so you simply **drop the question word into the sentence** where the answer would go. No reshuffling required. Here are all the essentials with examples.

## The Core Question Words

| English | Hangul | Romanization |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| who | 누구 | nugu |
| what | 무엇 / 뭐 | mueot / mwo |
| when | 언제 | eonje |
| where | 어디 | eodi |
| why | 왜 | wae |
| how | 어떻게 | eotteoke |
| which | 어느 | eoneu |
| how many / how much | 얼마나 / 몇 | eolmana / myeot |

In everyday speech, **뭐 (mwo)** is the casual, spoken form of **무엇 (mueot)**, "what." You will hear 뭐 far more often in conversation.

## How Question Word Order Works

Korean is Subject-Object-Verb, and questions keep that order. You place the question word where the answer would sit, and the verb stays at the end.

- Statement: **민수가 학교에 가요.** (Minsuga hakgyoe gayo.) — "Minsu goes to school."
- Question: **누가 학교에 가요?** (Nuga hakgyoe gayo?) — "Who goes to school?"

Notice you do **not** invert the sentence like English ("Does he...?"). You just swap in the question word and raise your intonation, or add the verb ending. This makes Korean questions refreshingly straightforward.

## Who — 누구

누구 means "who." When it acts as the subject, it combines with the subject particle 가 to become **누가 (nuga)**, not 누구가.

- **이분은 누구예요?** (ibuneun nuguyeyo?) — "Who is this person?"
- **누가 전화했어요?** (nuga jeonhwahaesseoyo?) — "Who called?"
- **누구를 만나요?** (nugureul mannayo?) — "Who are you meeting?"

## What — 무엇 / 뭐

Use **뭐** in speech and **무엇** in writing or formal contexts.

- **이게 뭐예요?** (ige mwoyeyo?) — "What is this?"
- **뭐 먹어요?** (mwo meogeoyo?) — "What are you eating?"
- **이름이 뭐예요?** (ireumi mwoyeyo?) — "What is your name?"

## When — 언제

언제 asks about time. No particle is needed in many cases, but you can add 에 for a specific point in time.

- **언제 와요?** (eonje wayo?) — "When are you coming?"
- **생일이 언제예요?** (saengiri eonjeyeyo?) — "When is your birthday?"
- **언제 한국에 갔어요?** (eonje hanguge gasseoyo?) — "When did you go to Korea?"

## Where — 어디

어디 asks about location. Pair it with **에 (e)** for direction/location or **에서 (eseo)** for where an action happens.

- **어디예요?** (eodiyeyo?) — "Where is it?"
- **어디에 가요?** (eodie gayo?) — "Where are you going?"
- **어디에서 일해요?** (eodieseo ilhaeyo?) — "Where do you work?"

## Why — 왜

왜 is short and stands alone easily. It is one of the most useful words to learn early.

- **왜요?** (waeyo?) — "Why?"
- **왜 안 와요?** (wae an wayo?) — "Why aren't you coming?"
- **왜 한국어를 배워요?** (wae hangugeoreul baewoyo?) — "Why are you learning Korean?"

## How — 어떻게

어떻게 asks "how" in the sense of method or manner. (For "how" describing a state, like "how is it," Korean uses 어때요.)

- **어떻게 가요?** (eotteoke gayo?) — "How do you get there?"
- **이거 어떻게 해요?** (igeo eotteoke haeyo?) — "How do you do this?"
- **어떻게 지내요?** (eotteoke jinaeyo?) — "How are you doing?"

## How Many / How Much — 몇 / 얼마

Korean splits "how much" by context:

| Question | Hangul | Use |
|----------|--------|-----|
| how many (counted) | 몇 | with counters: 몇 명, 몇 시 |
| how much (price) | 얼마 | cost of something |
| how much / to what degree | 얼마나 | extent, duration |

- **몇 시예요?** (myeot siyeyo?) — "What time is it?" (literally "how many hours")
- **이거 얼마예요?** (igeo eolmayeyo?) — "How much is this?"
- **얼마나 걸려요?** (eolmana geollyeoyo?) — "How long does it take?"

## A Handy Question-and-Answer Set

Practicing question and answer together makes these stick:

| Question | Sample answer |
|----------|---------------|
| 누구예요? (Who is it?) | 친구예요. (It's a friend.) |
| 뭐 해요? (What are you doing?) | 공부해요. (I'm studying.) |
| 언제 와요? (When are you coming?) | 내일 와요. (I'm coming tomorrow.) |
| 어디 가요? (Where are you going?) | 학교에 가요. (I'm going to school.) |
| 왜 그래요? (Why is that / what's wrong?) | 피곤해요. (I'm tired.) |
| 어떻게 가요? (How do you go?) | 버스로 가요. (I go by bus.) |

## Key Takeaways

- Core question words: **누구 (who), 뭐/무엇 (what), 언제 (when), 어디 (where), 왜 (why), 어떻게 (how)**.
- Korean keeps **statement word order** — just insert the question word where the answer goes.
- **누구 + 가** becomes **누가** as a subject.
- Use **뭐** in speech and **무엇** in writing.
- Split "how much": **몇** for counted amounts, **얼마** for price, **얼마나** for extent.
