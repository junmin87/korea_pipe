# Korean Batchim (받침): Final Consonants and Pronunciation

A **batchim (받침)** is the consonant that sits at the **bottom of a Korean syllable block** — the final consonant. Here is the key fact most learners miss: although Korean spells batchim with many different consonants, they are actually pronounced as only **seven possible sounds**. Master those seven, plus a few linking rules, and your pronunciation will jump forward dramatically.

## What "Batchim" Means

The word 받침 literally means "support" — the consonant that supports the block from below. In a block like **밥 (bap)**, the bottom ㅂ is the batchim. In **강 (gang)**, the bottom ㅇ is the batchim.

| Block | Batchim | Reads as |
|-------|---------|----------|
| 산 | ㄴ | san |
| 물 | ㄹ | mul |
| 밥 | ㅂ | bap |
| 강 | ㅇ | gang |

## The 7 Batchim Sounds

This is the heart of batchim. Many written consonants collapse into the **same spoken sound** when they land in the batchim position. There are only seven final sounds in Korean.

| Spoken sound | Written consonants that make it | Example | Reads as |
|--------------|----------------------------------|---------|----------|
| ㄱ (k) | ㄱ, ㅋ, ㄲ | 책 | chaek |
| ㄴ (n) | ㄴ | 손 | son |
| ㄷ (t) | ㄷ, ㅌ, ㅅ, ㅆ, ㅈ, ㅊ, ㅎ | 옷 | ot |
| ㄹ (l) | ㄹ | 발 | bal |
| ㅁ (m) | ㅁ | 봄 | bom |
| ㅂ (p) | ㅂ, ㅍ | 입 | ip |
| ㅇ (ng) | ㅇ | 방 | bang |

The surprising one is the **ㄷ group**: many different letters (ㅅ, ㅈ, ㅊ, ㅌ, ㅎ, and more) all become a clipped "t" sound at the end of a block. For example, 옷 (clothes) is pronounced "ot," not "os."

### Important: Batchim Sounds Are Unreleased

Final consonants in Korean are **not fully released** like in English. The "k" in 책 (chaek) is a stopped, held sound — your tongue moves into position but you do not push out a puff of air. The same is true for the final ㅂ and ㄷ sounds. They are clipped, not exploded.

## Double Batchim (겹받침)

Some syllables have **two consonants** in the batchim position, like 닭 (chicken) or 앉 (in 앉다, to sit). Normally **only one of the two is pronounced**.

| Word | Double batchim | Pronounced as | Meaning |
|------|----------------|---------------|---------|
| 닭 | ㄺ | 닥 (dak) | chicken |
| 삶 | ㄻ | 삼 (sam) | life |
| 값 | ㅄ | 갑 (gap) | price |
| 앉다 | ㄵ | 안따 (antta) | to sit |

Which consonant survives depends on the specific pair and whether a vowel follows. As a beginner, learn the common words individually rather than memorizing every rule.

## Key Pronunciation Rules Involving Batchim

Batchim does not stay still — it interacts with the next syllable. Three rules cover most cases.

### Rule 1: Liaison (Linking to a Following Vowel)

When a batchim is followed by a syllable that **starts with ㅇ (a vowel)**, the batchim **slides over** to become the start of that next syllable.

- **한국어** (hanguk + eo) → pronounced "han-gu-geo," because the ㄱ of 국 links into 어.
- **음악** (eum + ak) → "eu-mak," the ㅁ links forward.
- **밥을** (bap + eul) → "ba-beul."

This is why spoken Korean flows smoothly — final consonants jump to fill empty vowel slots.

### Rule 2: Nasalization

When certain stop sounds (ㄱ, ㄷ, ㅂ) meet a following ㄴ or ㅁ, they turn into nasal sounds.

- **한국말** → "한궁말" (hangungmal): the ㄱ becomes ㅇ before ㅁ.
- **입니다** → "임니다" (imnida): the ㅂ becomes ㅁ before ㄴ.

This is why the polite ending **-습니다** sounds like "seum-ni-da."

### Rule 3: The Silent / Weakening ㅎ

A batchim ㅎ often weakens or disappears, especially before a vowel.

- **좋아요** (joh + ayo) → "joayo," the ㅎ is dropped.
- **싫어요** (silh + eoyo) → "sireoyo."

## Common Mistakes

- **Over-releasing final consonants.** Keep them clipped and unreleased, not "kuh" or "puh."
- **Reading ㅅ/ㅈ/ㅊ batchim as written.** They all become a "t" sound at the end: 옷 = "ot," 낮 = "nat."
- **Ignoring liaison.** 한국어 is "han-gu-geo," not "han-guk-eo" with a hard stop.

## Key Takeaways

- A **batchim** is the final consonant at the bottom of a syllable block.
- Despite many spellings, there are only **7 spoken batchim sounds**.
- Final consonants are **unreleased** — clipped, not exploded.
- **Double batchim** usually pronounces only one of the two consonants.
- Watch for **liaison, nasalization, and weakening ㅎ** when batchim meets the next syllable.
