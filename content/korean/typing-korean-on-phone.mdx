# How to Type Korean on iPhone and Android

To type Korean on your phone, you add the **Korean keyboard** in your settings, then switch to it with the globe key while typing. Both iPhone and Android support Korean out of the box — no extra app needed. Below are the exact steps for each, plus how the two main Korean layouts work.

## Add the Korean Keyboard on iPhone

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Tap **General**, then **Keyboard**.
3. Tap **Keyboards**, then **Add New Keyboard...**
4. Scroll to and select **Korean (한국어)**.
5. Choose a layout — **2-Set (2벌식)** for the full QWERTY-style board, or **10 Key (천지인)** for the compact number-pad style.

To type, tap any text field, then press and hold the **globe icon** (🌐) to pick Korean, or tap it to cycle through your keyboards.

## Add the Korean Keyboard on Android

Steps vary slightly by brand (Samsung, Google Pixel, etc.), but the path is similar:

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Go to **General management** (Samsung) or **System** (Pixel).
3. Tap **Language and input**, then **On-screen keyboard**.
4. Choose your keyboard app (e.g., **Samsung Keyboard** or **Gboard**).
5. Tap **Languages**, then **Add keyboard / Add language**, and select **Korean (한국어)**.
6. Pick a layout: **2-Set (QWERTY)** or **Cheonjiin (천지인)**.

While typing, tap the **globe** or **language** key (often a small globe or 한/영 button) to switch between Korean and English.

## The Two Main Korean Layouts

You will be asked to choose between two layouts. Here is the difference:

| Layout | Korean name | Best for | How it feels |
|--------|-------------|----------|--------------|
| 2-Set | 2벌식 (dubeolsik) | Standard typing, larger screens | Each key is one letter, like a computer keyboard |
| 10 Key | 천지인 (cheonjiin) | One-handed, compact typing | Fewer keys; you combine strokes to build vowels |

### 2-Set (2벌식)

This is the standard Korean keyboard, the same layout used on computers. **Consonants sit on the left, vowels on the right.** You type a syllable letter by letter — first consonant, vowel, then any final consonant — and the phone automatically assembles them into a syllable block.

For example, to type **한** (han): tap ㅎ, then ㅏ, then ㄴ. The phone stacks them into one block as you go.

Double consonants like ㄲ, ㄸ, ㅃ, ㅆ, ㅉ are typed by holding **Shift** (the up-arrow) and pressing the base consonant.

### 10 Key / Cheonjiin (천지인)

This compact layout is built around three vowel strokes that represent "sky, earth, and human": a **dot (·)**, a **horizontal line (ㅡ)**, and a **vertical line (ㅣ)**. You combine these to form every vowel.

- ㅏ = ㅣ then ·
- ㅓ = · then ㅣ
- ㅗ = · then ㅡ
- ㅜ = ㅡ then ·

Consonants share keys; tapping the same key repeatedly cycles through related letters (for example, ㄱ → ㅋ → ㄲ). It takes practice but is fast and thumb-friendly once learned.

## Helpful Typing Tips

- **Auto-assembly:** You never type a finished syllable block directly — you type the individual letters in order and the phone builds the block. Just type left to right.
- **Switching languages quickly:** Most keyboards remember your last two keyboards, so a single tap of the globe key flips between Korean and English.
- **Korean voice input:** Add Korean as a recognized language and you can dictate in Korean using the microphone key.
- **Spacing matters:** Korean uses spaces between words (띄어쓰기). The space bar still works normally.
- **Predictive text:** Both Gboard and Samsung Keyboard offer Korean autocorrect and suggestions, which speed up common words.

## Troubleshooting

- **Globe key missing?** You need at least two keyboards installed for the switch key to appear. Add Korean, and the globe will show up.
- **Letters not combining?** Make sure you selected a Korean *typing* layout (2-Set or Cheonjiin), not just the language. Type letters in syllable order and they will merge.
- **Wrong autocorrect:** Turn off autocorrect for Korean in keyboard settings if it changes your intended words.

## Key Takeaways

- Both iPhone and Android include Korean keyboards — just add them in Settings.
- Switch keyboards with the **globe / language key** while typing.
- **2-Set (2벌식)** is the standard, computer-style layout — best for most users.
- **Cheonjiin (천지인)** is a compact, thumb-friendly layout that builds vowels from three strokes.
- Type letters in syllable order; the phone assembles the blocks for you.
