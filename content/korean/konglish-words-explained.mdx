# Konglish: Common English Loanwords in Korean

**Konglish (콩글리시)** is the nickname for English words that have been borrowed into Korean — often reshaped in pronunciation or meaning along the way. Some Konglish is just English written in Hangul, but some words have drifted so far that they confuse native English speakers. Learning Konglish gives you an instant vocabulary boost, as long as you know which words are "false friends."

## What Konglish Actually Is

Korean has absorbed thousands of English loanwords, especially for modern things like technology, food, and fashion. When spoken, these words follow **Korean sound rules**: English sounds that don't exist in Korean get swapped, and a vowel is often added so every syllable can be pronounced. The result sounds distinctly Korean even though the root is English.

For example, "coffee" becomes **커피 (keopi)** and "computer" becomes **컴퓨터 (keompyuteo)** — recognizable, but adapted.

## Easy Konglish You Already Know

Many loanwords keep their original meaning. Once you can read Hangul, you "know" these instantly:

| English | Konglish | Romanization |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| coffee | 커피 | keopi |
| computer | 컴퓨터 | keompyuteo |
| bus | 버스 | beoseu |
| taxi | 택시 | taeksi |
| ice cream | 아이스크림 | aiseukeurim |
| television | 텔레비전 | tellebijeon |
| internet | 인터넷 | inteonet |
| camera | 카메라 | kamera |
| hotel | 호텔 | hotel |
| juice | 주스 | juseu |

Reading these aloud is great pronunciation practice, since they show how Korean adapts English sounds.

## False Friends: Konglish That Means Something Different

This is where Konglish gets tricky. Some words look English but mean something unexpected, or are not used in English at all. Memorize these to avoid confusion.

| Konglish | Romanization | What it actually means | Note |
|----------|--------------|------------------------|------|
| 핸드폰 | haendeupon | cell phone | "hand phone," not used in English |
| 노트북 | noteubuk | laptop | "notebook" = laptop computer |
| 에어컨 | eeokeon | air conditioner | shortened "air con" |
| 아파트 | apateu | apartment building | refers to high-rise complexes |
| 원룸 | wonrum | studio apartment | "one room" |
| 셀카 | selka | selfie | "self camera" |
| 헬스 | helseu | gym / working out | "health" means the gym |
| 오피스텔 | opiseutel | studio office-apartment | "office" + "hotel" |
| 미팅 | miting | a group blind date | not a business meeting |
| 서비스 | seobiseu | something free / on the house | a freebie from the shop |

If a shop owner gives you something **서비스**, they mean it is **free** — a small gift, not a "service" you pay for.

## Konglish Built From English Parts

Some Konglish words are invented in Korea by combining English pieces in ways native speakers never would. They are perfectly normal Korean, just not real English.

| Konglish | Romanization | Meaning | "Made from" |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| 핸드폰 | haendeupon | cell phone | hand + phone |
| 백미러 | baengmireo | rearview mirror | back + mirror |
| 리모컨 | rimokeon | remote control | remo(te) + con(trol) |
| 에어컨 | eeokeon | air conditioner | air + con |
| 스킨십 | seukinsip | physical affection | skin + ship |
| 파이팅 | paiting | "You can do it!" / cheer | from "fighting" |

**파이팅 (paiting)** deserves a special mention — it is a hugely popular cheer of encouragement, like "Let's go!" or "You've got this!" It does not imply actual fighting.

## Food and Café Konglish

Cafés and restaurants are full of Konglish, so this set is very practical:

| English | Konglish | Romanization |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| cake | 케이크 | keikeu |
| sandwich | 샌드위치 | saendeuwichi |
| ice (iced drink) | 아이스 | aiseu |
| hot | 핫 | hat |
| set menu / combo | 세트 | seteu |
| takeout | 테이크아웃 | teikeuaut |
| size | 사이즈 | saijeu |

You can order surprisingly well by stringing these together, for example **아이스 아메리카노** (iced americano).

## How Konglish Pronunciation Adapts

A few patterns explain why English words sound the way they do in Korean:

- **No "f" or "v" sounds.** "f" becomes ㅍ (p) and "v" becomes ㅂ (b): coffee → 커피, video → 비디오.
- **Added vowels.** Korean syllables cannot end in many consonant clusters, so vowels (usually ㅡ "eu") get inserted: cream → 크림, ice → 아이스.
- **"r" and "l" merge.** Both map to ㄹ.

Recognizing these patterns lets you guess the Konglish form of many English words.

## Example Sentences

- **핸드폰 충전기 있어요?** (haendeupon chungjeongi isseoyo?) — "Do you have a phone charger?"
- **아이스 아메리카노 한 잔 주세요.** (aiseu amerikano han jan juseyo.) — "One iced americano, please."
- **이거 서비스예요.** (igeo seobiseuyeyo.) — "This one is on the house."
- **시험 잘 봐요, 파이팅!** (siheom jal bwayo, paiting!) — "Do well on your exam — you've got this!"

## Key Takeaways

- **Konglish (콩글리시)** is English borrowed and reshaped into Korean.
- Many words keep their meaning (커피, 버스, 택시) and are easy wins.
- Watch for **false friends**: 핸드폰 (cell phone), 노트북 (laptop), 서비스 (a freebie), 미팅 (blind date).
- Some words are **invented in Korea** from English parts (리모컨, 파이팅).
- Pronunciation adapts: no f/v sounds, added vowels, and r/l merge into ㄹ.
