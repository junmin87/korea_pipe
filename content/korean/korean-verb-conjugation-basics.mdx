# Korean Verb Conjugation Basics: Present, Past, and Future

Korean verb conjugation is far more regular than English. You start from the **verb stem**, then attach an ending for tense and politeness. There are very few irregular verbs to worry about at the beginning. This guide covers the **present, past, and future** tenses in the everyday polite (해요) style.

## Step 1: Find the Verb Stem

Every Korean verb in the dictionary ends in **다 (da)**. To get the stem, just remove 다.

| Dictionary form | Meaning | Stem |
|-----------------|---------|------|
| 가다 (gada) | to go | 가 |
| 먹다 (meokda) | to eat | 먹 |
| 보다 (boda) | to see | 보 |
| 하다 (hada) | to do | 하 |
| 마시다 (masida) | to drink | 마시 |

Everything you conjugate attaches to this stem.

## Step 2: The 아요 / 어요 Rule (Present Tense)

For the polite present tense, you add **-아요** or **-어요** to the stem. The choice depends on the **last vowel of the stem**:

- If the last vowel is **ㅏ or ㅗ**, add **-아요**.
- For **any other vowel**, add **-어요**.
- **하다** verbs become **해요** (a special, very common case).

| Stem | Last vowel | Ending | Present form | Meaning |
|------|-----------|--------|--------------|---------|
| 먹 | ㅓ | 어요 | 먹어요 | eat(s) |
| 좋 | ㅗ | 아요 | 좋아요 | is good / like(s) |
| 읽 | ㅣ | 어요 | 읽어요 | read(s) |
| 하 | (하다 verb) | 해요 | 해요 | do(es) |

### Vowel Contractions

When a stem ends in a vowel, it often **merges** with the ending so it is easier to say:

| Verb | Stem + ending | Spoken/written as | Meaning |
|------|---------------|-------------------|---------|
| 가다 | 가 + 아요 | 가요 | go |
| 보다 | 보 + 아요 | 봐요 | see |
| 마시다 | 마시 + 어요 | 마셔요 | drink |
| 오다 | 오 + 아요 | 와요 | come |

These contractions follow consistent patterns, and your ear quickly learns them.

## Step 3: Past Tense (았어요 / 었어요)

The past tense uses the **same ㅏ/ㅗ rule**, but the ending becomes **-았어요** or **-었어요**. Think of it as the present-tense vowel choice, plus 어요 shifted into the past.

- ㅏ/ㅗ stems → **-았어요**
- other vowels → **-었어요**
- 하다 verbs → **했어요**

| Verb | Present | Past | Meaning (past) |
|------|---------|------|----------------|
| 가다 | 가요 | 갔어요 | went |
| 먹다 | 먹어요 | 먹었어요 | ate |
| 보다 | 봐요 | 봤어요 | saw |
| 하다 | 해요 | 했어요 | did |
| 마시다 | 마셔요 | 마셨어요 | drank |

## Step 4: Future Tense (ㄹ/을 거예요)

The most common everyday future uses **-(으)ㄹ 거예요**, meaning "will" or "is going to."

- If the stem ends in a **vowel**, add **-ㄹ 거예요**.
- If the stem ends in a **consonant**, add **-을 거예요**.

| Verb | Stem ends in | Future form | Meaning |
|------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| 가다 | vowel (가) | 갈 거예요 | will go |
| 먹다 | consonant (먹) | 먹을 거예요 | will eat |
| 보다 | vowel (보) | 볼 거예요 | will see |
| 하다 | vowel (하) | 할 거예요 | will do |

There is also a "going-to / intention" future with **-(으)려고 해요**, but **-(으)ㄹ 거예요** is the workhorse to learn first.

## Full Conjugation Table

Here is 먹다 (to eat) and 가다 (to go) across all three tenses in the polite style:

| Tense | 먹다 (eat) | 가다 (go) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Present | 먹어요 | 가요 |
| Past | 먹었어요 | 갔어요 |
| Future | 먹을 거예요 | 갈 거예요 |

## Example Sentences

- **저는 매일 한국어를 공부해요.** (jeoneun maeil hangugeoreul gongbuhaeyo.) — "I study Korean every day." (present)
- **어제 친구를 만났어요.** (eoje chingureul mannasseoyo.) — "I met a friend yesterday." (past)
- **내일 영화를 볼 거예요.** (naeil yeonghwareul bol geoyeyo.) — "I will watch a movie tomorrow." (future)
- **점심을 먹었어요?** (jeomsimeul meogeosseoyo?) — "Did you eat lunch?" (past question)

## Making It Negative

To say "do not," put **안 (an)** before the verb, or attach **-지 않다**:

- **안 가요.** (an gayo.) — "I don't go."
- **먹지 않았어요.** (meokji anasseoyo.) — "I didn't eat."

Both are correct; 안 + verb is the quicker, more spoken option.

## A Few Common Irregulars

Most verbs follow the rules above, but a handful change their stem. Recognize these early:

| Verb | Type | Present form |
|------|------|--------------|
| 듣다 (to hear) | ㄷ irregular | 들어요 |
| 돕다 (to help) | ㅂ irregular | 도와요 |
| 살다 (to live) | ㄹ verb | 살아요 |

You do not need to master irregulars now — just be aware they exist so they do not surprise you.

## Key Takeaways

- Remove **다** to find the verb stem; conjugate from there.
- Present tense: add **-아요** (after ㅏ/ㅗ) or **-어요** (otherwise); 하다 → 해요.
- Past tense: **-았어요 / -었어요**; 하다 → 했어요.
- Future tense: **-(으)ㄹ 거예요** — vowel stem takes ㄹ, consonant stem takes 을.
- Negate with **안** before the verb.
