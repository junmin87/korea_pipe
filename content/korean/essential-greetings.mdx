# Essential Korean Greetings and Phrases

Knowing how to greet people is the first step to having real conversations in Korean. Korean has different levels of formality, so the greeting you choose depends on who you're talking to.

## Formal Greetings

These are safe to use with anyone — strangers, elders, colleagues, and in professional settings.

### 안녕하세요 (annyeonghaseyo) — Hello

The universal Korean greeting. Use it any time of day. It literally means "Are you at peace?" but functions exactly like "hello."

> **Tip:** The ㅎ in 하 is pronounced softly, so it sounds more like "an-nyeong-ha-se-yo" with a light "h."

### 감사합니다 (gamsahamnida) — Thank you

The formal way to say thank you. Use this as your default — it's never too formal.

### 죄송합니다 (joesonghamnida) — I'm sorry

A formal apology. Use this when you've made a mistake or need to be polite.

## Casual Greetings

Use these only with close friends, siblings, or people younger than you.

### 안녕 (annyeong) — Hi / Bye

The casual version of 안녕하세요. It works for both hello and goodbye among friends.

### 고마워 (gomawo) — Thanks

Casual thank you. Only use with close friends.

## Meeting and Parting

| Situation | Korean | Romanization | Meaning |
|-----------|--------|-------------|---------|
| Meeting someone | 만나서 반갑습니다 | mannaseo bangapseumnida | Nice to meet you |
| Goodbye (to person leaving) | 안녕히 가세요 | annyeonghi gaseyo | Go in peace |
| Goodbye (to person staying) | 안녕히 계세요 | annyeonghi gyeseyo | Stay in peace |
| See you later | 또 만나요 | tto mannayo | Let's meet again |

## Formality Levels Explained

Korean has several speech levels. For beginners, focus on two:

1. **Formal polite (합쇼체):** Endings like -습니다/-ㅂ니다. Use in business, news, presentations.
2. **Informal polite (해요체):** Endings like -아요/-어요. Use in everyday conversations with people you're not intimate with.

Both are respectful. The informal polite level (해요체) is what you'll use most in daily life.

## Practice Dialogue

> **A:** 안녕하세요! (Hello!)
>
> **B:** 안녕하세요! 만나서 반갑습니다. (Hello! Nice to meet you.)
>
> **A:** 저는 [your name]입니다. (I am [your name].)
>
> **B:** 반갑습니다! (Nice to meet you!)
