# Navigation Apps in Korea

If you've just arrived in Korea and opened Google Maps for driving directions, you've probably noticed something is wrong: **it doesn't give them**. This catches almost every foreigner off guard. The fix is simple — use **Naver Map** or **Kakao Map** instead. Here's what to know.

## Why Google Maps Doesn't Fully Work in Korea

Google Maps in Korea has real limitations compared to the rest of the world:

- **No driving directions.** Google Maps generally **cannot give turn-by-turn driving navigation** within Korea. This is due to long-standing restrictions on exporting detailed Korean mapping data to overseas servers.
- **Walking directions are limited and often unreliable.**
- **Search can be incomplete.** Many local businesses, exact entrances, and details are missing or out of date.
- **Public transit does work** reasonably well, so Google Maps is okay for subway/bus routing — but for anything else, it falls short.

The bottom line: **for driving and walking, use a Korean app.** Google Maps is at best a backup for transit.

## The Two Apps to Use: Naver Map and Kakao Map

| Feature | **Naver Map** | **Kakao Map** |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Driving navigation | Yes, full turn-by-turn | Yes, full turn-by-turn |
| Walking directions | Yes, reliable | Yes, reliable |
| Public transit | Yes, detailed | Yes, detailed |
| Business info & reviews | Extensive | Extensive |
| English support | Available (varies) | Available (varies) |

Both are excellent and free. Many foreigners install **both** — one may have better English search or detail for a given place. **Naver Map** is the most widely used overall; **Kakao Map** is equally capable and ties into Kakao's ecosystem (including Kakao T for taxis).

## English Support: What to Expect

Both apps offer **English interfaces**, but coverage isn't perfect:

- **Menus and directions** are largely available in English.
- **Place names and reviews** are often in Korean, since they're user-generated and business-supplied.
- **Searching** sometimes works better with the **Korean name** of a place. If an English search fails, try the Korean spelling, or search the address.

**Tip:** Set the app language to English in settings, but learn to copy-paste Korean place names (from a website or message) into the search bar for best results.

## How to Search Effectively

1. **Search by name** in English first; if that fails, try Korean.
2. **Search by address.** Korea uses a **road-name address system** (도로명주소). Pasting a full address is reliable.
3. **Use the place's phone number** as a search term — sometimes it pulls up the exact business.
4. **Tap the result** to see hours, reviews, photos, the exact entrance, and the "navigate here" button.

## Getting Directions

### Driving

- Enter your destination, choose the **car/driving** option, and follow turn-by-turn voice guidance.
- The apps account for **real-time traffic** and often suggest faster alternate routes.
- They show **speed cameras and limits** — useful given Korea's heavy camera enforcement.

### Public transport

- Choose the **transit** option to get subway + bus combinations, with **departure times, transfer points, and which subway exit to use**.
- Korean transit routing is excellent — it'll even tell you the **best car to board** for a fast transfer at some stations.

### Walking

- The **walking** option gives reliable pedestrian routes, including through complex areas and underground passages near major stations.

## Bonus: What These Apps Also Do

Naver Map and Kakao Map are more than maps — they're local discovery tools:

- **Business listings** with hours, menus, and photos
- **User reviews and ratings** (great for finding restaurants and clinics)
- **Street view / aerial view** in many areas
- **Bookmarking** favorite places
- **Indoor maps** for big malls and stations

This makes them genuinely useful for everyday life, not just getting from A to B.

## Calling a Taxi

For taxis, **Kakao T** (from Kakao) is the dominant ride-hailing app, integrating with Kakao Map. It lets you call a taxi to your location and see the route. Many foreigners pair Kakao Map for navigation with Kakao T for rides.

## Quick Setup Checklist

- **Install Naver Map and Kakao Map** (both are free).
- **Set the language to English** in each app's settings.
- **Practice searching by address** using Korea's road-name system.
- **Use Korean place names** when English search comes up empty.
- **Keep Google Maps only as a transit backup**, not for driving.
