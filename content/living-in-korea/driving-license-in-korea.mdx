# Driving License in Korea for Foreigners

If you want to drive in Korea, you have two practical paths: drive on an **International Driving Permit (IDP)** for a short stay, or **convert your foreign license to a Korean license** if you're living here. The right choice depends on how long you'll be in Korea.

## Quick Answer

| Your Situation | What You Need |
|----------------|---------------|
| Tourist or short visit (under ~1 year) | International Driving Permit from your home country |
| Resident with an ARC, staying long-term | Convert to a Korean driver's license |
| No home-country license at all | Take the full Korean driving test |

An IDP is only valid for a limited period (commonly up to one year from entry, depending on your nationality and the permit type). For anything longer, convert to a Korean license.

## Option 1: International Driving Permit (IDP)

An IDP is a translation of your existing license recognized under international convention. Korea accepts IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention.

### Key points

- You **must obtain the IDP in your home country before arriving** — Korea cannot issue one for a foreign license.
- An IDP is **not valid on its own**. You must carry it together with your original home-country license and your passport.
- IDPs are typically valid for **up to one year**. After that, you cannot keep renewing by leaving and re-entering — Korean authorities expect long-term drivers to convert.
- Rental car companies in Korea will ask to see the IDP, your original license, and your passport.

### Countries and the Geneva Convention

Most travelers from Geneva Convention countries (much of Europe, the US, Australia, and others) can use an IDP without issue. A few countries issue permits under a different convention; if yours does, check acceptance before relying on it. When in doubt, confirm with a Korean rental company or the **Korean Road Traffic Authority (도로교통공단)** before driving.

## Option 2: Converting a Foreign License to a Korean License

If you live in Korea, converting your existing foreign license is usually far easier than taking the full Korean test. This process is handled by a **driver's license examination office (운전면허시험장)**.

### Who can convert

Whether you can convert — and whether you must take a short written test — depends on a reciprocity agreement between Korea and the country that issued your license. Drivers from many countries can convert with minimal testing; others may need to pass a simple written exam.

### Documents you'll typically need

- Your **valid foreign driver's license** (must be currently valid, not expired)
- **Passport** and **Alien Registration Card (ARC)**
- **Passport-style photos** (bring a couple of spares)
- A **certificate or official confirmation of your license** in some cases (issued by your home authority or embassy), if the license alone can't be verified
- Completed application form (available at the exam office)
- A **medical/eyesight check**, usually done on-site

> Requirements vary by nationality and can change. Confirm the current document list with your local driver's license exam office before going.

### Conversion steps

1. **Gather your documents**, including any embassy verification your nationality requires.
2. **Visit a driver's license exam office.** Major cities have several; smaller areas have at least one regional office.
3. **Take the eyesight and basic physical check** on-site.
4. **Pass the written test if required** for your nationality (a short multiple-choice exam available in several languages, including English).
5. **Surrender your foreign license** — in many cases the office holds your original and returns it when you leave Korea, or issues a document acknowledging it.
6. **Receive your Korean license**, often the same day.

### What you usually avoid

When converting, you generally **skip the practical road test and the lengthy driving course** that first-time Korean applicants go through. That's the main advantage of converting rather than starting fresh.

## Option 3: Taking the Full Korean Test

If you don't hold a license from anywhere, you'll go through the standard Korean licensing process:

1. **Written exam** (available in English and other languages at major exam offices)
2. **Functional/skills test** on a closed course
3. **Road test** in real traffic
4. Mandatory **aptitude and eyesight checks**

Many foreigners use a licensed driving school (운전학원) to prepare, since instructors handle paperwork and provide practice vehicles.

## Practical Tips for Driving in Korea

- **Korea drives on the right**, with left-hand-drive vehicles, similar to the US and most of Europe.
- **Speed and traffic cameras are everywhere.** Fines are mailed to the registered driver or rental company, so violations in a rental will reach you.
- **Navigation is essential** — Google Maps does not give driving directions in Korea. Use Naver Map or Kakao Map (covered in our navigation guide below).
- **Carry your documents at all times.** Police may ask for your license, IDP, and passport during a check.
- **Drinking and driving is treated very seriously.** The legal blood-alcohol limit is low and enforcement is strict; penalties are severe.

## Which Option Should You Choose?

- **Visiting for a trip or a few months?** Get an IDP at home and bring your original license.
- **Moving to Korea with an ARC?** Convert your license once you're settled — it's valid long-term and avoids carrying multiple documents.
- **No license yet?** Plan for the full Korean test, ideally through a driving school.
