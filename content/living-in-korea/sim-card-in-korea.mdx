# SIM Card in Korea for Foreigners

Getting connected in Korea is straightforward. You can pick up a prepaid SIM at the airport within minutes of landing, or set up an eSIM before you even board your flight.

## Your Options at a Glance

| Option | Best For | Setup Time | Korean Number? |
|--------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Airport prepaid SIM | Tourists, short stays | 10 minutes | Yes |
| eSIM (Airalo, Ubigi, etc.) | Travelers who want instant setup | Instant | Usually no (data-only) |
| Carrier postpaid plan | Residents with ARC | 30 minutes | Yes |
| Portable Wi-Fi router | Groups, families | 5 minutes | No |

## Major Korean Carriers

Korea has three major telecom carriers:

| Carrier | Network Quality | Foreigner-Friendly? |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **SK Telecom (SKT)** | Largest network, best rural coverage | Tourist SIM available at airports |
| **KT (Korea Telecom)** | Strong coverage, popular with foreigners | Roaming/tourist SIM options at airports |
| **LG U+** | Good coverage, competitive pricing | Tourist SIM available at airports |

All three offer excellent 4G LTE and 5G coverage in urban areas. For most visitors, the difference between carriers is negligible in cities like Seoul, Busan, and Incheon.

## Option 1: Buy a Prepaid SIM at the Airport

The easiest option for tourists. Prepaid SIM counters are located in the arrivals hall at **Incheon International Airport** (both Terminal 1 and Terminal 2) and **Gimpo Airport**.

### What You Need

- **Passport** (required for all SIM purchases in Korea)
- **Credit or debit card** (cash also accepted)

### What You Get

Tourist SIM packages typically include:

- **Data:** Unlimited data (with speed throttling after a daily or total cap) or a set data allowance
- **Calls/texts:** Some packages include domestic calls and texts; data-only packages are also available
- **Duration:** 5, 10, 20, or 30 days — choose based on your stay

### Typical Pricing

- **5-day data-only:** ₩20,000–30,000 (~$15–23 USD)
- **10-day with calls:** ₩35,000–45,000 (~$27–35 USD)
- **30-day unlimited data:** ₩55,000–80,000 (~$42–60 USD)

Prices vary by carrier and package. Check the carrier booths at the airport for current offerings.

### Where to Buy at Incheon Airport

- **Terminal 1:** SIM card counters on the 1st floor (Arrivals), near gates 6–8. Look for SKT, KT, and LG U+ booths.
- **Terminal 2:** SIM counters on the 1st floor (Arrivals), near gate 4.
- **Hours:** Counters typically open from early morning until the last flight arrives.

**Tip:** Lines can be long during peak arrival times. If you're in a hurry, consider an eSIM instead.

## Option 2: eSIM (No Physical Card Needed)

If your phone supports eSIM, this is the most convenient option — set it up before you fly.

### Popular eSIM Providers for Korea

| Provider | Data | Duration | Approximate Price |
|----------|------|----------|-------------------|
| **Airalo** | 1–20 GB | 7–30 days | $5–26 USD |
| **Ubigi** | 1–50 GB | 1–30 days | $4–30 USD |
| **Holafly** | Unlimited data | 5–90 days | $19–84 USD |
| **eSIM Korea (KT)** | Various | 5–30 days | ₩15,000–60,000 |

### eSIM Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Instant activation — no waiting in airport lines
- Keep your home SIM active alongside the eSIM
- Easy to top up or extend

**Cons:**
- Most eSIMs are data-only (no Korean phone number)
- Not all phones support eSIM — check your device first
- Without a Korean phone number, you can't verify Korean apps (KakaoTalk, Naver, delivery apps)

### Does Your Phone Support eSIM?

Most recent iPhones (XS and later) and many Android flagships (Samsung Galaxy S20+, Google Pixel 3+) support eSIM. Check your phone's settings under **Cellular/Mobile Data** — if you see an option to add a plan, you're good.

## Option 3: Postpaid Plan (For Residents)

If you have an **Alien Registration Card (ARC)**, you can sign up for a regular Korean phone plan.

### Requirements

- ARC (외국인등록증)
- Passport
- Korean bank account (for auto-payment setup)

### Process

1. Visit a carrier store (SKT, KT, or LG U+ — found in every neighborhood)
2. Bring your ARC and passport
3. Choose a plan (typically ₩30,000–70,000/month for unlimited data)
4. The store will set up your SIM and activate your number

### Budget-Friendly MVNOs

If you want to save money, Korea has many MVNOs (Mobile Virtual Network Operators) that use the big three carriers' networks at lower prices:

- **Tplus** (SKT network)
- **KT M Mobile** (KT network)
- **U+ 알뜰모바일** (LG U+ network)
- **Mint Mobile Korea / HeRO** (various networks)

MVNO plans can start as low as ₩10,000–20,000/month for basic data packages.

## Option 4: Portable Wi-Fi Router

If you don't need a phone number and are traveling in a group, a portable Wi-Fi device (also called "pocket Wi-Fi" or "egg") lets multiple devices share one connection.

- **Where to rent:** Airport counters, online pre-order through Klook or KKday
- **Price:** ₩3,000–8,000/day (~$2–6 USD)
- **Return:** Drop it off at the airport counter when you leave

## Which Option Should You Choose?

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tourist, 1–2 weeks | eSIM (if your phone supports it) or airport prepaid SIM |
| Tourist, needs Korean number for apps | Airport prepaid SIM with calls |
| Traveling in a group | Portable Wi-Fi router |
| Resident with ARC | Postpaid plan or MVNO |
| Short business trip | eSIM for data + international calling app |

## Important Tips

- **Korean apps need a Korean number.** Many Korean services (KakaoTalk registration, food delivery apps, Naver verification) require a Korean phone number for signup. If you plan to use these, get a SIM with a Korean number.
- **Free Wi-Fi is everywhere.** Korea has extensive free Wi-Fi in subway stations, cafes, and public spaces. If you only need occasional connectivity, you might manage with Wi-Fi alone.
- **Unlocked phone required.** Prepaid SIMs and eSIMs only work with unlocked phones. Check with your home carrier before traveling.
