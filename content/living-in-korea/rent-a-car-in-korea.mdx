# Renting a Car in Korea as a Foreigner

Renting a car in Korea opens up the countryside, coastlines, and places public transit doesn't reach. The process is straightforward **if you have the right license and documents**. Here's the practical guide for foreigners.

## What You Need to Rent

To rent a car in Korea, you'll generally need:

- **A valid driver's license** — either an **International Driving Permit (IDP)** paired with your home license, or a **Korean driver's license**
- **Your original home-country license** (the IDP is not valid alone)
- **Passport** (for identification)
- **A credit card** in the driver's name (for payment and the security hold)
- To meet the **minimum age** requirement (rental companies often require drivers to be over a certain age, frequently 21+, sometimes higher for larger vehicles)

> The **IDP must be obtained in your home country before arriving** — Korea cannot issue one for a foreign license. Bring the IDP, your original license, and your passport to the rental counter. See our driving license guide below for the IDP-vs-Korean-license decision.

## License Rules in Brief

| Your License | Can You Rent? |
|--------------|---------------|
| IDP (Geneva Convention) + home license | Yes, within the IDP's validity period |
| Korean driver's license | Yes |
| Home license only, no IDP | Usually no — most companies require an IDP or Korean license |

Korea recognizes IDPs issued under the **1949 Geneva Convention**. If your country issues a different type, confirm acceptance with the rental company before booking.

## Where and How to Rent

- **Major rental companies** operate at **airports** (Incheon, Gimpo, Jeju, and others) and in cities. Jeju Island in particular has a huge rental market, since a car is the best way to explore it.
- **Book in advance**, especially in peak seasons and on Jeju — availability tightens and prices rise.
- **English-friendly booking** is available through international rental brands and some Korean platforms; airport counters are used to foreign customers.

### Booking steps

1. **Reserve online or at a counter**, choosing your car class and rental period.
2. **Present your documents** — IDP (or Korean license), original license, passport, and credit card.
3. **Review the insurance options** (see below) and decide your coverage.
4. **Inspect the car** with staff and **photograph any existing damage** before driving off.
5. **Confirm the fuel policy** (usually return with the same level) and drop-off location.

## Insurance: Don't Skip This

Korean rentals offer tiered insurance, and choosing the right level matters:

| Coverage | What It Does |
|----------|--------------|
| **Basic/mandatory** | Legally required liability coverage, usually included |
| **Collision damage waiver (CDW)** | Reduces your liability for damage to the rental car |
| **Full / premium coverage** | Lowers or removes the deductible and broadens protection |

- **Read the deductible (self-pay) amount.** Even with CDW, you may owe a fixed amount in an accident unless you buy fuller coverage.
- **Check what credit-card or travel insurance already covers** to avoid paying twice — but confirm it's accepted in Korea.
- For peace of mind on unfamiliar roads, many foreigners opt for **fuller coverage**.

## Driving in Korea: What to Know

- **Drive on the right**, with left-hand-drive cars (like the US and most of Europe).
- **Navigation is essential and Korean.** Google Maps **does not give driving directions** in Korea — use **Naver Map** or **Kakao Map**. Many rentals include a built-in Korean navigation system; set it to English if possible.
- **Cameras everywhere.** Speed and red-light cameras are extremely common. **Fines from a rental reach you** via the rental company, so drive within limits.
- **Tolls.** Expressways use electronic tolling (Hi-pass). Ask whether your rental has a Hi-pass device and how tolls are billed, or be ready to pay at toll booths.
- **Parking** can be tight and paid in cities; apps and the navigation system help find lots.
- **Strict drink-driving laws.** The blood-alcohol limit is low and enforcement is heavy — never drive after drinking.

## Costs to Expect

Rental pricing depends on car class, season, rental length, and insurance level. Beyond the base rate, budget for:

- **Insurance upgrades** (often the biggest add-on)
- **Fuel** (refuel before returning if that's the policy)
- **Tolls** on expressways
- **Parking** in cities

> Specific rates vary widely by company, season, and location. Compare a couple of providers and read what the quoted price includes (especially insurance) before booking.

## Tips for a Smooth Rental

- **Bring your IDP, original license, and passport** — all three.
- **Set up Naver Map or Kakao Map** before you drive off.
- **Photograph the car's condition** at pickup and drop-off.
- **Understand your insurance deductible**, not just that you "have insurance."
- **Mind the speed cameras** — they're the most common way foreigners rack up fines.
- **Return on time and refueled** to avoid extra charges.

## Is Renting Worth It?

In **cities like Seoul**, public transport is so good that a car is often more hassle than help. But for **Jeju Island, the coast, national parks, and rural regions**, a rental car is the best — sometimes only — practical way to explore. Match the choice to your itinerary.
