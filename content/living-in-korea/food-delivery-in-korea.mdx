# Food Delivery Apps in Korea for Foreigners

Korea has one of the most developed food delivery cultures in the world. You can get almost anything delivered — full restaurant meals, convenience store items, coffee, and groceries — usually within 30–60 minutes. Here's how to use the major delivery apps as a foreigner.

## The Big Three Apps

| App | Korean Name | English Support | Best For |
|-----|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **Baemin** | 배달의민족 | Partial (improving) | Widest restaurant selection |
| **Coupang Eats** | 쿠팡이츠 | Partial | Fast delivery, good UI |
| **Yogiyo** | 요기요 | Limited | Budget-friendly options |

## Baemin (배달의민족) — The Market Leader

Baemin is Korea's most popular delivery app with the largest restaurant network. The name means "Delivery People" (배달의 민족, literally "nation of delivery").

### Setting Up Baemin

**Requirements:**
- Korean phone number (for SMS verification)
- Download from App Store or Google Play

**Registration steps:**
1. Download **배달의민족** from your app store
2. Open the app and select **회원가입** (Sign Up)
3. Enter your Korean phone number for verification
4. Set your delivery address (in Korean — use Naver Map to find the Korean address of your location)

### English Support

Baemin has been expanding English support, but the experience is still primarily in Korean. Some tips:

- **Menu photos:** Most restaurants include photos of every dish, so you can browse visually even without reading Korean
- **Papago or Google Translate:** Use the camera translation feature to translate menu descriptions in real-time
- **Favorites:** Once you find dishes you like, save them for easy reordering

### Payment Methods

- Korean debit/credit cards
- KakaoPay
- Naver Pay
- Toss Pay
- Some international cards may work for in-app payment (results vary)

## Coupang Eats (쿠팡이츠) — Fast and User-Friendly

Coupang Eats is run by Coupang, Korea's largest e-commerce company. It's known for fast delivery times and a clean interface.

### Why Foreigners Often Prefer Coupang Eats

- **Slightly better English support** than competitors
- **Clean, intuitive interface** — easier to navigate even in Korean
- **Real-time delivery tracking** with accurate ETAs
- **Coupang integration** — if you already use Coupang for shopping, you have an account

### Setting Up Coupang Eats

1. Download **Coupang Eats** (쿠팡이츠)
2. Sign up with your Korean phone number or existing Coupang account
3. Set your delivery address
4. Browse and order

### Payment Methods

- Korean cards
- International credit cards (Visa, Mastercard — more reliable than on other apps)
- Coupang Pay
- Toss Pay

## Yogiyo (요기요) — Budget Option

Yogiyo (meaning "here!" or "over here!") is the third major player. It's known for frequent discount coupons and promotions.

### English Support

Yogiyo has the least English support of the three. Expect a fully Korean interface. However, the app layout is straightforward and similar to other delivery apps, so the learning curve is manageable with some practice.

### Setting Up Yogiyo

1. Download **요기요** from your app store
2. Register with a Korean phone number
3. Set your delivery address
4. Browse by category or search

## How Korean Food Delivery Works

### Ordering Process (All Apps)

1. **Set your address** — the app shows restaurants that deliver to your location
2. **Browse restaurants** — filter by cuisine type, rating, delivery time, or minimum order
3. **Select dishes** — add items to your cart
4. **Minimum order** — most restaurants require a minimum order amount (usually ₩10,000–15,000)
5. **Checkout** — choose payment method and confirm
6. **Track delivery** — real-time tracking shows when your food will arrive

### Delivery Fees

| Fee Type | Typical Range |
|----------|--------------|
| **Basic delivery fee** | ₩0–4,000 (~$0–3 USD) |
| **Distance surcharge** | Additional ₩1,000–3,000 for far locations |
| **Late-night surcharge** | Additional ₩1,000–2,000 after ~9 PM |
| **Rain/snow surcharge** | Additional ₩1,000–2,000 during bad weather |

Many restaurants offer free delivery above a certain order amount.

### Tipping

**Korea does not have a tipping culture.** Do not tip the delivery driver — it's not expected and can cause confusion. The delivery fee covers the driver's service.

### Translation Tricks

1. **Screenshot + Translate:** Take a screenshot of the menu and use Google Translate or Papago's image translation
2. **Copy-paste:** Long-press Korean text in the app to copy it into a translator
3. **Photo menus:** Rely on dish photos — most popular restaurants include clear images
4. **Order popular items:** Sort by "인기메뉴" (popular menu) or "많이 주문한" (most ordered) to see what others are getting

## The "No Korean Number" Problem

All Korean delivery apps require a Korean phone number for registration. If you're a tourist without a Korean number:

### Workarounds

1. **Get a SIM with a Korean number** — see our [SIM card guide](/living-in-korea/sim-card-in-korea)
2. **Ask your hotel or Airbnb host** — some hosts will order food for you
3. **Use hotel delivery services** — many hotels can order delivery on your behalf
4. **Walk-in alternatives** — use Naver Map or Google Maps to find nearby restaurants

### For Residents

Once you have an ARC and Korean bank account, setting up delivery apps is straightforward. Link your Korean card for seamless payments.

## Beyond Restaurant Delivery

Korean delivery goes far beyond food:

| Service | App | What It Delivers |
|---------|-----|-----------------|
| **Grocery delivery** | Coupang, Market Kurly | Groceries, often by next morning |
| **Convenience store delivery** | Baemin, GS25 app | Convenience store items in 30 min |
| **Coffee delivery** | Baemin, Coupang Eats | Cafe drinks from local shops |
| **Late-night delivery** | Baemin 1 (배민원) | Convenience items, snacks, medicine |

**Market Kurly** (마켓컬리) deserves special mention — it's a premium grocery delivery service that delivers by 7 AM if you order by 11 PM the night before ("새벽배송" or dawn delivery).

## Which App Should You Use?

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| First time, want it easy | **Coupang Eats** — best interface, decent English support |
| Want the most restaurant choices | **Baemin** — largest selection |
| On a budget, want deals | **Yogiyo** — frequent coupons |
| Need groceries | **Coupang** (main app, not Eats) or **Market Kurly** |

**Pro tip:** Install all three. Restaurant availability and delivery fees differ between apps, so compare before ordering.
