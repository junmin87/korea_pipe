# ATMs in Korea for Foreigners

Korea is one of the most cashless-friendly countries in the world, but you'll still need cash sometimes — small restaurants, market stalls, and taxis occasionally require it. The challenge: not every Korean ATM accepts foreign cards.

## Which ATMs Accept Foreign Cards?

Most Korean bank ATMs do **not** accept international cards. Here are the ones that reliably do:

| ATM Location | Networks Accepted | Hours | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|-------|
| **Global ATMs (하나은행)** | Visa, Mastercard, Amex, JCB, UnionPay | 24/7 (most) | Found in airports, tourist areas, subway stations |
| **Shinhan Bank Global ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard, JCB, UnionPay | Bank hours + some 24/7 | Look for "Global" ATM label |
| **CU convenience store ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard | 24/7 | Widely available; English interface |
| **GS25 convenience store ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard | 24/7 | Growing international card support |
| **7-Eleven ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard | 24/7 | Common in Seoul and major cities |
| **Woori Bank Global ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard, UnionPay | Bank hours vary | Airport and tourist locations |
| **Korea Post Office ATMs** | Visa, Mastercard, Cirrus | Business hours | Found nationwide, even in rural areas |

### The Most Reliable Option

**Convenience store ATMs** (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven) are your best bet for foreign card access because:
- They're everywhere — tens of thousands across Korea
- Open 24/7
- Most have an English language option
- They consistently support international Visa and Mastercard

## How to Use a Korean ATM with a Foreign Card

1. Insert or swipe your card
2. Select **English** (or your preferred language)
3. Choose **Withdrawal**
4. Select **Savings/Checking** (even if it's a different account type at home)
5. Enter the amount in Korean won (₩)
6. Confirm and take your cash

**Tip:** If the ATM asks about currency conversion (DCC — Dynamic Currency Conversion), **always choose to be charged in Korean won (KRW)**, not your home currency. DCC adds a markup of 3–7%.

## Fees and Limits

### ATM Fees

| Fee Type | Typical Amount |
|----------|---------------|
| **Korean ATM operator fee** | ₩1,000–3,000 per withdrawal (~$0.75–2.30 USD) |
| **Your bank's international ATM fee** | $2–5 per withdrawal (varies by bank) |
| **Foreign transaction fee** | 1–3% of the amount (charged by your bank) |

**Total cost per withdrawal:** Expect to pay roughly $3–8 in combined fees, depending on your bank.

### Withdrawal Limits

| Limit Type | Typical Amount |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Per transaction** | ₩500,000–1,000,000 (~$380–760 USD) |
| **Daily limit** | ₩2,000,000–3,000,000 (~$1,500–2,300 USD) |

Limits vary by ATM operator and your home bank's settings. Check with your bank before traveling.

### How to Minimize Fees

1. **Withdraw larger amounts less frequently** — one big withdrawal costs less in fees than several small ones
2. **Use a travel-friendly bank card** — banks like Charles Schwab, Wise, and Revolut reimburse ATM fees and charge no foreign transaction fees
3. **Decline DCC** — always choose to pay in KRW, not your home currency
4. **Check your bank's partner networks** — some banks have fee-free ATM agreements in Korea

## Paying Without Cash

Korea is extremely card-friendly. You can use your international credit or debit card at most:

- Convenience stores
- Department stores and malls
- Restaurants (most mid-range and above)
- Subway ticket machines
- Hotels

**Where cash is still needed:**
- Traditional markets (시장)
- Small street food vendors
- Some taxis (though most now take cards)
- Neighborhood restaurants (especially outside Seoul)
- Coin-operated machines (laundry, lockers)

### Contactless and Mobile Pay

- **Apple Pay:** Supported in Korea since 2023, works at most card terminals via Hyundai Card partnership
- **Samsung Pay:** Widely accepted
- **Google Pay:** Limited acceptance
- **KakaoPay / Naver Pay:** Require a Korean bank account or card to set up

## Opening a Korean Bank Account

If you're living in Korea, a local bank account makes life much easier — needed for rent payments, salary deposits, and Korean payment apps.

### Requirements

| Document | Required? |
|----------|----------|
| **Passport** | Yes |
| **ARC (Alien Registration Card)** | Yes, for most banks |
| **Korean phone number** | Yes |
| **Proof of employment or enrollment** | Some banks require it |

### Where to Open an Account

| Bank | English Support | Foreigner-Friendly? |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| **KEB Hana Bank** | Good | Yes — dedicated foreigner services |
| **Shinhan Bank** | Good | Yes — English banking app |
| **Woori Bank** | Moderate | Yes |
| **KB Kookmin Bank** | Moderate | Yes, but less English support |
| **NH Bank (Nonghyup)** | Limited | Possible but challenging |

**Best options for foreigners:** Hana Bank and Shinhan Bank have the most foreigner-friendly processes and English-language support.

### Fintech Alternatives

| Service | Can Foreigners Use It? | What It Does |
|---------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Toss** | Yes (with ARC) | Mobile banking, transfers, payments |
| **KakaoBank** | Yes (with ARC) | Online-only bank, easy app-based setup |
| **K Bank** | Yes (with ARC) | Online bank, cryptocurrency integration |

These app-based banks often have simpler sign-up processes than traditional banks, but still require an ARC.

### Prepaid Tourist Cards

If you don't have an ARC, prepaid travel cards are an excellent alternative to opening a bank account:

- **WOWPASS** — A prepaid card designed for tourists. Load it with cash at airport kiosks or convenience stores, then use it like a debit card at most Korean merchants. Also works for transit. Available at Incheon Airport and major tourist areas.
- **Namane Card** — Another prepaid tourist card option with similar functionality.

## Currency Exchange Tips

While not strictly about ATMs, you'll likely need to exchange some cash:

| Method | Rate | Convenience |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Airport exchange** | Worst rates | Most convenient |
| **Banks** | Good rates | Moderate convenience |
| **Licensed money changers** | Best rates | Found in Myeongdong, Dongdaemun, Itaewon |
| **ATM withdrawal** | Good rates (bank exchange rate + fees) | Very convenient |

**Best strategy:** Withdraw a small amount from the airport ATM for immediate needs, then exchange larger amounts at money changers in the city or simply use ATMs throughout your trip.
