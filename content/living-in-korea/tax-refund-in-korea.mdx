# Tax Refund in Korea for Foreigners

Korea offers a VAT refund on purchases made by foreign tourists. If you're not a Korean resident, you can claim back the 10% VAT on eligible purchases — potentially saving you a significant amount on shopping.

## Who Is Eligible?

You can claim a tax refund if you meet **all** of these conditions:

- You are **not a Korean citizen** (or are a Korean citizen living abroad for 2+ years)
- You have stayed in Korea for **less than 6 months**
- You are taking the purchased goods **out of Korea**
- You bought from a **tax-free participating store**

**Not eligible:** Foreign residents with an ARC who have been in Korea for more than 6 months.

## The Basics

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **VAT rate** | 10% |
| **Minimum purchase** | ₩15,000 per transaction at a single store |
| **Maximum per item** | ₩500,000 for immediate refund; higher amounts processed at airport |
| **Refund amount** | Approximately 7–10% of the purchase price (after processing fees) |
| **Required document** | Passport |

## Two Ways to Get Your Refund

### Method 1: Instant Refund at the Store (Tax-Free Shopping)

Many stores in Korea are part of the **immediate tax refund** system. You get the discount right at the register.

**How it works:**

1. Look for the **"Tax Free"** or **"Tax Refund"** logo at the store entrance
2. At checkout, show your **passport**
3. The store deducts the VAT from your total immediately
4. You pay the reduced price — no need to visit the airport refund counter

**Limits:** Instant refunds are available for individual purchases up to ₩500,000 and cumulative purchases up to ₩2,500,000 per trip.

**Where you'll find it:** Major department stores (Lotte, Shinsegae, Hyundai), cosmetics shops (Olive Young, Innisfree), fashion retailers, and many tourist-area shops.

### Method 2: Refund at the Airport

For purchases where you didn't get an instant refund, or for amounts above the instant refund limit, claim your refund at the airport.

**Step-by-step at Incheon Airport:**

1. **Before check-in:** If your refund items are going in checked luggage, visit the **Customs Declaration Desk** (located before security) to get your items inspected and stamped. This is for purchases over ₩2,000,000.
2. **After security/immigration:** Visit a **tax refund kiosk** or **refund counter** in the departure area.
3. **Scan your receipts** at the automated kiosk, or hand them to the counter staff.
4. **Choose your refund method:** cash (Korean won), credit card refund, or Alipay/WeChat Pay.

### Refund Kiosk Locations at Incheon Airport

- **Terminal 1:** Near gates 25–28 (after immigration)
- **Terminal 2:** Near gates 249–253 (after immigration)
- **Customs desks:** Located on the 3rd floor (departure level) before check-in counters

## Major Tax Refund Operators

Korea has several tax refund operators. The logo on your receipt tells you which kiosk or counter to use:

| Operator | Where to Find Kiosks |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Korea Tax Free (KTFS)** | Incheon, Gimpo, major airports |
| **Global Tax Free** | Incheon, Gimpo, Busan |
| **Global Blue** | Incheon Airport |
| **Easy Tax Refund** | Incheon Airport, select locations |

**Tip:** Different operators have different kiosks. Check the logo on your receipt and match it to the correct kiosk at the airport.

## What You Can (and Can't) Claim

### Eligible Items
- Clothing and fashion accessories
- Cosmetics and skincare
- Electronics (phones, cameras, accessories)
- Handbags and shoes
- Korean traditional goods and souvenirs
- Food and snacks (packaged, for takeout)

### Not Eligible
- Services (hotels, restaurants, transportation)
- Items consumed in Korea
- Tobacco and alcohol (separate duty-free system)
- Items shipped separately from your departure

## How Much Will You Actually Get Back?

The refund isn't a full 10% because operators charge a processing fee. Here's a rough guide:

| Purchase Amount (₩) | Approximate Refund (₩) | Effective Rate |
|---------------------|----------------------|----------------|
| 15,000–49,999 | 1,500 | ~3–10% |
| 50,000–74,999 | 3,500 | ~5–7% |
| 75,000–99,999 | 5,000 | ~5–7% |
| 100,000–199,999 | 7,000–14,000 | ~7% |
| 200,000–499,999 | 15,000–36,000 | ~7–8% |
| 500,000+ | ~7–8% of purchase | ~7–8% |

For large purchases, the effective refund rate approaches the full 10%.

## Tips to Maximize Your Refund

1. **Consolidate purchases.** Buy ₩15,000+ per transaction at a single store to qualify. Small purchases below the threshold don't count.
2. **Use instant refund when possible.** It's faster, and you avoid airport lines.
3. **Keep your receipts organized.** Sort by refund operator to speed up airport processing.
4. **Don't open or use refund items.** Customs may inspect items — they must be unused and in original packaging.
5. **Arrive early at the airport.** Refund kiosk lines can be very long, especially during peak seasons and holidays.
6. **Use the automated kiosks.** They're faster than the staffed counters and available in English, Chinese, and Japanese.

## Downtown Refund Options

Some neighborhoods have **downtown tax refund centers** where you can claim refunds before going to the airport:

- **Myeongdong** — multiple refund offices in the main shopping area
- **Dongdaemun** — refund counters in major shopping malls

Downtown refunds require a credit card hold (charged if you don't depart Korea with the goods within the refund period).

## Duty-Free Shops (Different System)

Duty-free shops (면세점) are a separate system from the tax refund. At duty-free shops, you pay without VAT or duties upfront.

| Type | How It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Airport duty-free** | Shop after immigration; take items on the plane |
| **Downtown duty-free** (Lotte, Shilla, etc.) | Shop in the city, pick up at the airport |
| **Online duty-free** | Order online, pick up at airport |

You can combine both systems — shop at duty-free stores for luxury goods and claim tax refunds on other purchases.
