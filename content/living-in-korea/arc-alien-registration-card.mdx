# Alien Registration Card (ARC) in Korea

The **Alien Registration Card (ARC)** is the ID card for foreigners living in Korea. If you're staying longer than 90 days, you almost certainly need one. It unlocks nearly everything in daily life: opening a bank account, signing a phone contract, getting national health insurance, and signing a lease.

## Who Needs an ARC?

You generally need to register for an ARC if you:

- Hold a **long-term visa** (work, study, family, residence, etc.), and
- Plan to **stay in Korea for more than 90 days**.

Short-term visitors and tourists do **not** get an ARC. Your visa type determines your eligibility and the documents you'll need.

## When to Apply

You must apply **within 90 days of your arrival** in Korea (some visa categories require it sooner, so check the conditions tied to your specific visa). Don't wait until the last minute — appointment slots fill up, especially in large cities.

## Where to Apply

Applications are handled by a local **immigration office (출입국·외국인청/사무소)**. You must apply at the office that covers your registered address. Appointments are booked through the **Hi Korea** immigration portal (hikorea.go.kr).

## Documents You'll Need

The exact list depends on your visa type, but the common core is:

- Completed **application form** (available at the office or downloadable from Hi Korea)
- Your **passport**
- One or more **passport-style photos** (check the current size requirement)
- The **application fee** (paid at the office)
- **Proof of address** in Korea — for example, a lease/rental contract, a dormitory confirmation, or a residence confirmation letter
- **Supporting documents tied to your visa** — for example, a certificate of employment for work visas, a certificate of enrollment for student visas, or a marriage/family document for family visas

> Document requirements vary by visa category and can change. Always confirm the current checklist for your specific visa on Hi Korea before your appointment.

### A note on health checks

Some visa categories (for example certain teaching and long-stay categories) require a **medical examination** as part of registration. If yours does, complete it at an approved hospital and bring the results.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply

1. **Confirm your address is settled.** You need a real Korean address and usually proof of it. Sort out housing first.
2. **Book an appointment on Hi Korea.** Create an account at hikorea.go.kr, select your local immigration office, and reserve a date. Slots can be limited, so book early.
3. **Gather your documents.** Use the visa-specific checklist and bring originals plus copies.
4. **Attend your appointment.** Submit your form and documents, pay the fee, and provide **fingerprints** (biometric registration is standard for most applicants).
5. **Wait for processing.** Your card is produced over the following weeks.
6. **Collect or receive your card.** Depending on the office, you'll pick it up or have it mailed to your registered address.

## How Long Does It Take?

Processing typically takes **a few weeks** from your appointment to receiving the card. During busy seasons it can take longer. In the meantime, keep your application receipt — it serves as proof that your registration is in progress.

## What's On the Card

Your ARC shows:

- Your **registration number** (a 13-digit ID used across Korean systems, similar in function to a resident number)
- Your **name, nationality, and date of birth**
- Your **visa/status of stay**
- A **photo** and **issue date**

This registration number is requested constantly — for banking, phone plans, deliveries, and online sign-ups — so keep your card safe and your number handy.

## After You Get Your ARC

The ARC is the key that unlocks the rest of resident life:

- **Bank account:** Most banks require an ARC to open a full account.
- **Phone contract:** A long-term SIM/phone plan needs your ARC.
- **National Health Insurance:** Many residents are enrolled automatically or can register once they have an ARC.
- **Leases and utilities:** Landlords and providers use your ARC number for contracts.

## Keeping Your ARC Valid

- **Report address changes.** If you move, you must report your new address to immigration (often possible online via Hi Korea or at a local district office) within the required window.
- **Renew before expiry.** Your card's validity is tied to your visa. Apply to extend your stay before the card or visa expires.
- **Carry it.** Foreign residents are generally expected to carry their ARC for identification.
- **Report loss promptly.** If your card is lost or stolen, apply for a reissue.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Booking late.** Appointment slots disappear fast in Seoul and other big cities — reserve as soon as you have an address.
- **Wrong office.** You must use the immigration office that covers your registered address.
- **Incomplete documents.** Missing a visa-specific item (like an employment certificate) can mean rebooking. Double-check the list on Hi Korea.
- **Letting it lapse.** An expired ARC or overstayed visa creates serious problems — track your dates.
